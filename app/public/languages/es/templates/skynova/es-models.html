    Capacity
    • Power Supply System

      The batteries in the station will primarily receive power from solar panels installed on the station's roof. In the event that the solar panels do not generate sufficient power, the system will automatically switch to the main power grid to ensure uninterrupted energy supply.

      Battery Backup

      In case of a power outage or adverse weather conditions that affect solar panel efficiency, the batteries are designed to provide backup power for 6 to 8 hours. This ensures that the station remains operational even during emergencies or unfavorable conditions.

    Main Features
    • An operator gets a call to his station and after which he assigns the closest drone/drones to a call. Drones will receive the gps coordinates through a computer.

      Those that are assigned to a call turn on while the doors are being opened and carefully fly out of the hangar.

      After the call has been completed, they return to the hangar. Once in the designated are, the doors will open via either gps, RF control or proximity sensors

      The drone/drones carefully land on the charging pad where they will charge until they receive another call.
