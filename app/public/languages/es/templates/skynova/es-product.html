Starlink

Starlink is a satellite internet provider that can give access to the internet wherever devices are. It aims to deliver high-speed internet access using a constellation of low Earth orbit (LEO) satellites, so we can use the internet in the jungle and even on the mars.

Fiware

Fiware isn't a typical internet provider, but more an open-source platform for developing smart solutions in the context of smart cities and the Internet of Things (IoT). Fiware is used to build applications and services in areas such as smart cities, agriculture, and industrial IoT.

Communication equipment

A well-designed network is essential for data transfer, involving devices like routers, switches, and cables. For drones with cameras, high-quality video transmitters and receivers are needed for reliable live streaming or recording. A stable wireless internet connection is crucial to transmit the video to the server.

Energy for stacion

A station requires constant electricity, so a grid and battery system, including a UPS, are essential. If the power supply fails, the UPS automatically provides power to devices. For charging drones, a Battery Management System (BMS) is used. The BMS monitors and manages drone battery health, ensuring safe charging, discharging, and optimal performance.

Drone controlling

A drone base must be controlled by a person through a Ground Control Station (GCS), which includes a computer, monitors, and control interfaces for flight planning, monitoring, and control. The flight controller is essential for managing the drone's stability, orientation, and navigation. Reliable and feature-rich flight controllers are recommended. GPS receivers are necessary for accurate positioning and navigation, with advanced systems like GNSS providing improved accuracy.

Servers and computers in the station

All drones, devices at the station, and drone operators need a connection to a main server. This server must be powerful and support multiple services including Flight Control, Communication, Telemetry Data Handling, Obstacle Avoidance, Security and Authentication, User Interface, Firmware and Software Updates, Logging and Analytics, Health Monitoring, and Emergency Response.

Components

Flight Management & Power Supply

Operators assign drones to calls, which then receive coordinates, execute tasks, and return to the hangar.
The station uses the main power grid and has emergency batteries powered by solar panels, providing 6-8 hours of backup.

Safety & Central Computer

Electrical safety features include surge protection and fire prevention mechanisms.
Central computer assists in drone assignment and tasks, with options for fiber optic connectivity.

Gates & Charging Modules

Aluminum roller gates are powered by electric motors, integrated with the central computer.
Computer Modules use QI wireless charging stations.

Space Organization & Security

Data and power are managed via Ethernet cables.
Drones are equipped with cameras, motion detection, and biometric authentication using Aldahua ASI6214S systems.
